SECTION 1: STUDENT NOTES (CLEAR, SIMPLE, ACTIONABLE)
================================================================================

KEY IDEA 1 – PICK ONE STRATEGY AND MASTER IT  
WHY IT MATTERS:  You can’t be good at everything.  Mastery gives you an edge over the crowd.  
HOW TO APPLY:  Choose the Minervini SEPA approach, study it daily, and ignore other “systems”.

KEY IDEA 2 – VALUATION IS SECONDARY TO MOMENTUM  
WHY IT MATTERS:  A high‑PE stock that is making new highs shows strong institutional demand; a low‑PE may hide trouble.  
HOW TO APPLY:  When a stock meets all technical qualifiers, take it even if the PE is double the market.

KEY IDEA 3 – TREND IS THE QUALIFIER, NOT THE PREDICTOR  
WHY IT MATTERS:  Buying a continuation of an existing up‑trend raises the probability of a big move.  
HOW TO APPLY:  Scan only for stocks above their 200‑day moving average (MA) and with Relative Strength (RS) > 68.

KEY IDEA 4 – FUNDAMENTALS HELP BUT ONLY IF PRICE CONFIRMS  
WHY IT MATTERS:  Earnings, sales, and margin growth are the fuel for a move, but the stock must be “telling” the story on the chart.  
HOW TO APPLY:  Look for earnings acceleration, margin expansion, and revenue growth **and** a breakout on price.  If the chart is weak, ignore the numbers.

KEY IDEA 5 – THREE WAYS A COMPANY GROWS EARNINGS  
1) Sell more units  
2) Raise prices  
3) Cut costs (margin improvement) – limited in duration.  
WHY IT MATTERS:  Sustainable growth comes from #1 and #2 (revenue growth).  
HOW TO APPLY:  Prioritize stocks with strong revenue growth; treat cost cuts as a short‑term boost only.

KEY IDEA 6 – MATURATION CYCLE & STAGE CONCEPTS  
STAGE 2 – Early uptrend, forming a base (VCP‑type contraction).  
STAGE 3 – Mid‑trend, growth still strong but beginning to slow.  
STAGE 4 – Late trend, earnings decelerating, price often below 200‑day.  
WHY IT MATTERS:  The best entry is at the start of Stage 2; exit before Stage 4.  
HOW TO APPLY:  Identify the base, buy on the breakout, and set a stop just below the base.

KEY IDEA 7 – POST‑EARNINGS DRIFT & ESTIMATE REVISIONS  
WHY IT MATTERS:  Analysts revise estimates slowly; a strong earnings surprise can keep the stock rising for weeks.  
HOW TO APPLY:  After a big positive surprise, stay in the trade if price holds above the breakout level; watch for a reversal in volume.

KEY IDEA 8 – DIFFERENTIAL DISCLOSURE (PRICE VS. FUNDAMENTALS)  
WHY IT MATTERS:  A huge earnings beat followed by a massive sell‑off signals that institutions are dumping despite the numbers.  
HOW TO APPLY:  If price moves opposite the earnings story (big down‑volume on a beat), stay out.

KEY IDEA 9 – CODE MATRIX (ACCELERATION SCORE)  
CODE 33 = 3 quarters of EPS acceleration + 3 quarters of revenue acceleration + 3 quarters of margin expansion (ideal).  
CODE 32, 23, etc. = partial combos.  
WHY IT MATTERS:  Higher code → higher probability of a big move.  
HOW TO APPLY:  Rank your watchlist by code; prioritize CODE 33 and CODE 32 stocks.

KEY IDEA 10 – DISCIPLINE & BELIEF ARE THE REAL EDGE  
WHY IT MATTERS:  Everyone has information; only disciplined traders stick to the plan and compound returns.  
HOW TO APPLY:  Follow the daily checklist, respect stop‑losses, and keep a trade journal to reinforce the process.

SECTION 2: MINERVINI TRADING CONCEPTS EXTRACTED
================================================================================

- **Market Leader / Institutional Favorite** – Stocks that dominate their niche; they attract the biggest capital flows.  
- **Turnaround Situation** – Companies with recent earnings acceleration after a period of weakness; look for sequential EPS jumps.  
- **Cyclicals & Commodities** – Generally avoided; they are less predictable and often lack the clean up‑trend needed for SEPA.  
- **Valuation (PE Ratio)** – High PE is acceptable if the stock is making new highs; low PE can be a red flag for hidden problems.  
- **Trend as Qualifier** – Only trade stocks in a confirmed up‑trend (above 200‑day MA, RS > 68).  
- **Earnings Growth Drivers** – Sell more, raise prices, cut costs (cost cuts are short‑lived).  
- **Maturation Cycle Stages** – Stage 2 (base formation), Stage 3 (mid‑trend), Stage 4 (late‑trend, deceleration).  
- **Post‑Earnings Drift** – Continued price rise after a strong earnings surprise due to slow analyst revisions.  
- **Differential Disclosure** – Look for mismatches between fundamentals and price action (e.g., big beat + huge sell‑off).  
- **Breakout Year** – A year where earnings or estimates jump out of a multi‑year range; signals acceleration.  
- **Code Matrix (Code 33, 32, 23, etc.)** – Scoring system for quarters of EPS, revenue, and margin acceleration.  
- **Profit‑Margin Expansion + Revenue Growth** – The “accelerant” that creates exponential earnings growth.  
- **Position Sizing & Risk** – Implicit: max 1% of capital per trade, stop‑loss just below the base.  
- **Psychology** – Discipline, belief in the process, avoidance of “forecasting” and “story‑telling”.

SECTION 3: TRADING RULES & DO'S/DON'TS
================================================================================

DO:  
- Trade only stocks above their 200‑day moving average.  
- Require Relative Strength (RS) greater than 68 before entering.  
- Look for a clear base (volatility contraction) and buy on the breakout.  
- Confirm earnings, sales, or margin acceleration with a price breakout.  
- Use the Code 33/32 matrix to prioritize watchlist candidates.  
- Set a stop‑loss just below the base or the most recent swing low.  
- Limit each trade to no more than 1% of total capital risk.  
- Review every trade in a journal; note why you entered and exited.  
- Keep discipline: no “forecasting” or buying on a story alone.  
- Re‑scan the market each morning for new Stage 2 setups.

DON’T:  
- Buy a stock that is below its 200‑day MA, even if fundamentals look great.  
- Trust earnings beats when the stock sells off on massive volume (differential disclosure).  
- Chase low‑PE “value” stocks that are in a downtrend.  
- Enter a trade when RS is below 68 or when the stock is in Stage 3/4.  
- Add to a losing position; instead, exit and re‑evaluate.  
- Over‑trade; stick to the pre‑defined number of setups per day.  
- Let emotions dictate position size; always respect the stop‑loss.  
- Ignore margin erosion; it often precedes earnings deceleration.

SECTION 4: SETUP IDENTIFICATION GUIDE (BASED ON TRANSCRIPT)
================================================================================

CHART CUES  
- Price above 200‑day MA (long‑term uptrend).  
- Relative Strength (RS) > 68, preferably in the 90‑99 range.  
- A recent base or VCP (tightening range, decreasing volume).  
- Breakout candle closing above the base high with increased volume.  

PRICE / VOLUME BEHAVIOR  
- Volume spikes on breakout → confirmation.  
- Large volume down‑move on an earnings beat = red flag (institutional dump).  
- Post‑earnings drift: price holds above breakout for several days/weeks.  

BUY POINTS  
- Enter on the first close above the base high (or on a pull‑back to the breakout level).  
- Ensure earnings/revenue/margin acceleration is present (check last 3‑4 quarters).  

CONFIRMATION SIGNALS  
- EPS, revenue, and margin each showing acceleration (Code 33 or at least Code 32).  
- Positive guidance revision or beat of the highest analyst estimate.  
- No sign of margin erosion or earnings deceleration.  

DANGER SIGNS / INVALIDATIONS  
- Price falls back below the 200‑day MA.  
- RS drops below 68.  
- Earnings beat accompanied by a large down‑volume day.  
- Margin compression or earnings deceleration in the last two quarters.  
- Stock entering Stage 3/4 (price plateau, base flattening).

SECTION 5: PRACTICAL DAILY ACTION PLAN FOR ME
================================================================================

PRE‑MARKET CHECKLIST  
- Update watchlist with stocks that meet: 200‑day MA up, RS > 68, recent base, Code 33/32.  
- Scan earnings calendar; flag any upcoming reports for watchlist stocks.  
- Verify the last 3‑4 quarters: EPS acceleration, revenue growth, margin expansion.  
- Set alerts for breakout levels and for any earnings surprise news.  

MARKET HOURS DISCIPLINE  
- Watch the pre‑market for any overnight news that could invalidate a setup.  
- When a stock breaks its base, enter on the first close above the breakout with volume confirmation.  
- Place stop‑loss immediately (2‑3% below entry or just below the base).  
- Size the position to risk ≤ 1% of total capital.  
- Monitor volume and price action; if price drops below stop, exit without hesitation.  
- Limit to a maximum of 3 new entries per day to avoid over‑trading.  

POST‑MARKET REVIEW TASKS  
- Log every trade: entry, exit, stop level, reason for entry, outcome.  
- Review any earnings releases that occurred; note differential disclosure signals.  
- Update the Code matrix for each watchlist stock (add new quarters, drop stale ones).  
- Identify any missed setups and why they were missed (e.g., late entry, wrong RS).  

RISK CONTROL REMINDERS  
- Never risk more than 1% of equity on a single trade.  
- Stop‑loss must be placed before the trade is executed.  
- If cumulative daily loss reaches 3% of capital, stop trading for the day.  
- Avoid trading stocks that fall below the 200‑day MA at any time.  

MINDSET REINFORCEMENT (MARK STYLE)  
- Remind yourself: “The chart is the boss; fundamentals are the assistant.”  
- Visualize the trade plan before the market opens; repeat the plan mentally.  
- Accept that losses are part of the process; focus on the win‑rate and expectancy.  
- Keep a “discipline mantra”: *“Stick to the rules, trust the price, compound fast.”*

SECTION 6: EXAMPLES (IF APPLICABLE)
================================================================================

1. **Crocs** – EPS +144% beat, but massive down‑volume on the day; price fell 20% despite the beat.  Lesson: ignore the earnings story when price shows institutional dumping.  

2. **Bicor (1991 IPO)** – Strong early earnings, then deceleration; price fell sharply after a modest beat.  Lesson: watch for earnings deceleration and price reaction.  

3. **Home Depot (example)** – RS = 90, growth 4% → 23% after breakout; earnings beat and guidance raised; stock doubled while PE stayed flat.  Lesson: high RS + earnings acceleration = big move.  

4. **Cisco (1990 IPO)** – Bought at IPO base, sold a few points higher, then the stock exploded 70,000%.  Lesson: early base breakout can yield massive compounding if the company sustains growth.  

5. **Apple Turnaround** – Margins rose from 1.2% to 23.9% (20×) with revenue growth → massive price appreciation.  Lesson: simultaneous margin and revenue expansion creates explosive earnings acceleration.  

6. **Monster Beverage** – Sales rose 20→63→93, margins jumped 3.3%→18%; earnings surged 75%–214% → stock exploded.  Lesson: profit‑margin expansion magnifies revenue growth.  

7. **Ollie’s** – Revenue +16.8%, margins +2.0% → earnings +50%; without margin boost earnings would be only ~8%.  Lesson: even a small margin increase can dramatically boost earnings when revenue is growing.

SECTION 7: FINAL 10‑POINT CHEAT SHEET
================================================================================

1. ONLY BUY ABOVE THE 200‑DAY MOVING AVERAGE – price must respect the long‑term trend.  
2. RELATIVE STRENGTH MUST BE > 68 (ideally 90+).  
3. ENTER ON THE BREAKOUT OF A VCP/BASE WITH INCREASING VOLUME.  
4. CONFIRM THAT EPS, REVENUE, AND MARGINS ARE ACCELERATING (CODE 33 or CODE 32).  
5. A HIGH PE IS OK IF THE STOCK IS MAKING NEW HIGHS; LOW PE MAY SIGNAL TROUBLE.  
6. IF PRICE SLOWS OR FALLS AFTER A BIG EARNINGS BEAT (large down‑volume), STAY OUT – differential disclosure.  
7. SET STOP‑LOSS JUST BELOW THE BASE OR 2‑3% BELOW ENTRY; RISK ≤ 1% OF CAPITAL.  
8. EXIT BEFORE STAGE 4 (earnings deceleration, margin erosion, price below 200‑day).  
9. COMPUND QUICKLY: roll gains into the next qualified breakout; avoid holding a stock for a full year to get a 20% move.  
10. DISCIPLINE OVER ALL – follow the checklist, trust the chart, and keep belief in the process.
