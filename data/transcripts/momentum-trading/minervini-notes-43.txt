SECTION 1: STUDENT NOTES (Clear, Simple, Actionable)
================================================================================

Key Idea 1 – POSITION SIZING BASICS  
Why it matters: Determines how much of your account is at risk on each trade. Too large a size blows up the account; too small limits profit potential.  
How to apply:  
- Use a max stop of 8% for a full position. If you stagger the stop (split the trade), you may allow 10‑12% on the portion that is farther from entry.  
- Keep average loss per trade around 3‑6% of the position.  
- Never exceed 25‑30% of your account on a single overnight position; 50% is an absolute ceiling (only in rare cases).  

Key Idea 2 – RISK OF TOTAL EQUITY (RTE)  
Why it matters: RTE translates position size + stop distance into a percentage of your whole account, letting you control overall drawdown.  
How to apply:  
- Target 1.0‑1.5% RTE per trade. 2.5% is the absolute max for experienced traders.  
- Example: 12.5% position size with a 10% stop = 1.25% RTE (good).  
- If you double the position size, you must halve the stop distance to keep the same RTE.  

Key Idea 3 – STOP‑LOSS DISCIPLINE  
Why it matters: Stops protect you from catastrophic loss and keep the RTE in check.  
How to apply:  
- When a stop is hit, exit immediately – no rationalizing, no “wait and see”.  
- Even for high‑quality stocks (e.g., Coca‑Cola) you must obey the stop.  

Key Idea 4 – POSITION SIZING OVER TIME (Pyramid)  
Why it matters: Small early positions protect capital while you learn; larger positions increase return when you have a proven edge.  
How to apply:  
- Start with “pilot” trades (tiny size, risk <0.5% RTE) to test the waters.  
- As you prove consistency, increase to the optimal 20‑35% of your account per trade (Kelly‑optimal for a 2:1 reward‑to‑risk).  

Key Idea 5 – POST‑TRADE ANALYSIS & GRADE  
Why it matters: Identifies strengths, weaknesses, and recurring tendencies. Without it you repeat the same mistakes.  
How to apply:  
- After each trade, mark up the chart and assign grades: perfect entry, early/late entry, perfect exit, early/late exit, missed opportunity, bad buy, bad sell.  
- Use a journal (digital or paper) to note why you made each decision.  
- Review trades after 1 month, 3 months, 6 months to remove emotional bias.  

Key Idea 6 – KNOW YOUR TENDENCIES  
Why it matters: Everyone has systematic biases (e.g., buying early, selling early). Recognizing them lets you guard against them.  
How to apply:  
- Track the frequency of each grade. If “early seller” appears >30% of exits, create a rule to always take partial profits instead of exiting fully.  
- Adjust your plan to counter the bias (e.g., set a rule to “sell only half at 20% profit, let the rest run”).  

Key Idea 7 – PROBABILITY THINKING VS ONE‑TIME GAINS  
Why it matters: Trading is a series of many small edges, not a lottery ticket. A 1% chance of losing everything will eventually happen if you take many trades.  
How to apply:  
- Favor a guaranteed modest return (e.g., $1,000 guaranteed) over a high‑risk jackpot.  
- Keep the “bell‑curve” mindset: aim for a high win‑rate with controlled loss, not occasional huge wins.  

Key Idea 8 – TRADE SETUP FILTERS (Recap of earlier days)  
Why it matters: Only trade stocks that meet the SEPA criteria, increasing the odds of a big winner.  
How to apply:  
- Must be in a Stage‑2 uptrend, above the 200‑day SMA for at least 30 days.  
- Look for VCP (volatility contraction) patterns, strong earnings growth, accelerating sales, and high relative strength.  
- Avoid stocks below the 200‑day SMA unless they are a proven “power play” with clear breakout potential.

SECTION 2: MINERVINI TRADING CONCEPTS EXTRACTED
================================================================================

- POSITION SIZING: 8% max stop for full position, 10‑12% for staggered portions; 25‑30% max account exposure overnight; 1‑1.5% RTE target.  
- RISK OF TOTAL EQUITY (RTE): Position size × stop % = % of total equity at risk. Keep ≤2.5% (ideal ≤1.5%).  
- STOP‑LOSS DISCIPLINE: Exit immediately when stop is hit; no exceptions.  
- KELLY / OPTIMAL POSITION SIZE: For a 2:1 reward‑to‑risk trader, ~25% of equity is optimal.  
- VCP (VOLATILITY CONTRACTION PATTERN): Tight price range with decreasing volume on the right side, indicating a breakout point.  
- STAGE‑2 UPTREND: Market‑leader condition; price above 200‑day SMA for ≥30 days.  
- RELATIVE STRENGTH: Must be among top performers in its sector/industry.  
- EARNINGS & SALES ACCELERATION: Look for sequential earnings growth and sales acceleration; margins alone are insufficient.  
- POWER PLAY: Early‑stage breakout stocks that may be below 200‑day SMA but have a clear catalyst and strong volume.  
- POST‑TRADE ANALYSIS: Grade each trade, track tendencies, use journal, review after weeks/months.  
- TENDENCY IDENTIFICATION: Recognize patterns like “early buyer”, “early seller”, “cut loss too soon”.  
- PROBABILITY THINKING: Treat trading as a series of small edges; avoid lottery‑style risk.

SECTION 3: TRADING RULES & DO'S/DON'TS
================================================================================

DO:
- Set a stop loss before entering any trade.
- Keep RTE between 1.0% and 1.5% (max 2.5% for very experienced traders).
- Use staggered stops when splitting a position; allow a slightly larger % stop on the later leg.
- Limit any single overnight position to ≤30% of account equity; never exceed 50% under any circumstance.
- Grade every trade immediately after exit and record notes in a journal.
- Review graded trades after at least one month to remove emotional bias.
- Trade only stocks in a Stage‑2 uptrend above the 200‑day SMA (or qualified power plays).
- Look for VCP patterns, accelerating earnings, and strong sales growth before entering.
- Adjust position size upward only after consistent performance (e.g., 80%+ perfect loss cuts).
- Take partial profits on strong moves; never sell the entire position at the first profit target.

DON’T:
- Ignore a stop once it’s hit; no “wait and see” or “the company is solid” excuses.
- Use a stop larger than 8% on a full‑size position unless you have staggered the trade.
- Overexpose your account (≥50% on one stock) – it creates a high risk of ruin.
- Trade stocks below the 200‑day SMA without a clear power‑play catalyst.
- Rely on a single big win (lottery mindset) to fund your strategy.
- Skip post‑trade analysis; it is the single biggest differentiator between winners and losers.
- Let emotions dictate scaling in/out; follow the pre‑defined plan instead.
- Forget to record why you made a trade; lack of notes makes future analysis impossible.

SECTION 4: SETUP IDENTIFICATION GUIDE (Based on Transcript)
================================================================================

Chart Cues:
- Price above 200‑day SMA for at least 30 consecutive days.
- Clear Stage‑2 uptrend: higher highs, higher lows, strong relative strength.
- Volatility Contraction Pattern (VCP): narrowing price range, decreasing volume on the right side of the pattern.
- Pivot point with a “tight entry zone” (price‑volume vacuum) right before breakout.

Price/Volume Behavior:
- Volume spikes on breakout candle, then contracts during pull‑back.
- Low volume on the right side of the VCP indicates accumulation.
- After breakout, volume should stay above the average of the last 20 days.

Buy Points:
- Enter at the low of the VCP or at the breakout candle if price breaks cleanly above the contraction zone.
- For staggered entries, place a smaller position first, then add on a second leg with a slightly wider stop (10‑12%).

Confirmation Signals:
- Relative Strength Index (RSI) above 70 is acceptable if price is still in an uptrend.
- Earnings or sales acceleration in the last two quarters.
- No major negative news catalyst.

Danger Signs / Invalidations:
- Price falls below the 200‑day SMA after entry.
- Stop‑loss hit on the first leg (indicates the pattern failed).
- Volume surge on the downside (selling pressure) before breakout.
- Unexpected earnings miss or downgrade that contradicts the growth narrative.

SECTION 5: PRACTICAL DAILY ACTION PLAN FOR ME
================================================================================

PRE‑MARKET CHECKLIST:
- Review overnight news for any earnings, guidance, or macro events affecting your watchlist.
- Verify that each stock on the watchlist is above its 200‑day SMA and in Stage‑2 uptrend.
- Scan for VCP patterns on the daily chart (tight price range, low right‑side volume).
- Update your journal with any new notes on why a stock is on the list.
- Set alerts for price levels where you would place entry or stop orders.

MARKET HOURS DISCIPLINE:
- Only enter trades that meet all SEPA criteria; skip anything that fails a single filter.
- Place stop‑loss order immediately after entry (calculate RTE to stay ≤1.5%).
- Use staggered entries for larger positions: first leg 2‑3% of equity, second leg up to 8‑10% with a wider stop.
- Monitor position size; never let any single stock exceed 30% of equity overnight.
- If a trade hits stop, exit instantly; do not re‑enter the same day.

POST‑MARKET REVIEW TASKS:
- Grade each trade (entry timing, exit timing, profit/loss, adherence to plan).
- Record the grade and a brief note on why the trade performed as it did.
- Update the “tendency” tracker (early buyer, early seller, etc.).
- Calculate daily RTE total; ensure it stayed within the 1‑2% range.
- Review any missed opportunities and note why you didn’t take them.

RISK CONTROL REMINDERS:
- RTE per trade ≤1.5% (max 2.5% for very experienced days).
- Max overnight exposure per stock ≤30% of equity.
- Total daily exposure (sum of all open positions) ≤70% of equity.
- Never trade without a pre‑defined stop.

MINDSET REINFORCEMENT (Mark style):
- Remind yourself: “I am managing risk, not chasing profit.”
- Visualize the bell‑curve: many small edges beat one big gamble.
- Accept that a few losses are inevitable; focus on the overall win rate.
- Keep the “integrity of the personal bell curve” front‑and‑center.
- Use the “respect the pause” mantra when you feel the urge to act impulsively.

SECTION 6: EXAMPLES (If Applicable)
================================================================================

Example 1 – Proper Position Sizing:  
- Account equity $100,000. Desired RTE 1.25%.  
- Choose 12.5% position size = $12,500.  
- Set stop at 10% of entry price → $1,250 risk.  
- $1,250 / $100,000 = 1.25% RTE – within target.

Example 2 – Bad Sell (Power Play)  
- Bought a power‑play at $50, price rose to $60 (+20%).  
- Sold the entire position immediately, missing further upside.  
- Correct approach: sell 30‑50% at $60, move stop to breakeven on remaining shares, let the rest run.

Example 3 – Post‑Trade Grading  
- Trade A: Entered early VCP, stopped out at 15% loss. Grade = “Bad sell – stop hit, but stop was correct”.  
- Trade B: Entered perfect VCP, exited at 30% profit, kept half position, moved stop to breakeven. Grade = “Excellent – perfect entry, partial profit‑take, good risk management”.
