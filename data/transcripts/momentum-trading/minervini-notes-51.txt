SECTION 1: STUDENT NOTES (Clear, Simple, Actionable)
================================================================================

KEY IDEA: Pre‑market routine and focus list creation  
WHY IT MATTERS: Gives you a disciplined start, filters thousands of symbols to a handful of viable candidates before the market opens.  
HOW TO APPLY: Every morning at 8:45 AM EST run the “Bread & Butter” screen, add any tight, low‑volume right‑side pullbacks to a WATCH LIST, then move promising symbols to ON‑DECK and finally to the BUY LIST once a clear pivot forms.

KEY IDEA: VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern) and right‑side tightening  
WHY IT MATTERS: A VCP shows supply being absorbed; the tighter the price range and the lower the volume, the higher the probability of a breakout.  
HOW TO APPLY: Look for 3‑5 consecutive lower lows on the daily, decreasing volume, and a clear “pivot” line where the price has stopped falling. The buy point is just above the breakout high (or the prior day’s high).

KEY IDEA: Stage‑2 uptrend (new bull market phase)  
WHY IT MATTERS: Stocks in a confirmed stage‑2 uptrend have momentum and are less likely to reverse on a pullback.  
HOW TO APPLY: Verify the stock is at least 25 % above its 52‑week low, above the 200‑day moving average, and has been trending higher for 4‑5 months on the weekly chart.

KEY IDEA: Fab‑5 panel (RPR, TPR, Pressure, Trend, Buy‑Risk)  
WHY IT MATTERS: Provides a quick, at‑a‑glance health check. Green across the board = strong candidate.  
HOW TO APPLY: Use the platform’s Fab‑5 panel; require RPR ≥ 90, TPR = A or B, Pressure off (no recent down pressure), Trend = Stage‑2, and Buy‑Risk green before entering.

KEY IDEA: Relative Performance Rank (RPR)  
WHY IT MATTERS: Equivalent to IBD’s Relative Strength but on a 1‑100 scale; higher numbers indicate market‑leading stocks.  
HOW TO APPLY: Filter for RPR ≥ 90; treat anything below 70 as a “no‑go.” Track RPR changes; a sharp rise can signal a power‑play opportunity.

KEY IDEA: Technical Profile Rank (TPR)  
WHY IT MATTERS: Scores how closely a stock’s technical profile matches historic winners.  
HOW TO APPLY: Aim for TPR = A or B. If the stock is a C, verify other Fab‑5 components before proceeding; D is off‑limits.

KEY IDEA: Pressure indicator  
WHY IT MATTERS: Shows short‑term buyer/seller dominance. A stock under pressure after a breakout often stalls.  
HOW TO APPLY: After a breakout, look for pressure to drop to green within 1‑2 days. If pressure stays red, consider exiting or tightening stops.

KEY IDEA: Power‑play criteria  
WHY IT MATTERS: Power plays are rare, high‑reward setups where fundamentals are less important because a catalyst is driving the move.  
HOW TO APPLY: Look for a stock that has doubled (or more) in ≤ 8 weeks, RPR ≥ 95, and a tight VCP or base. Accept higher volatility but keep stop‑loss tight (≤ 2 % of entry price).

KEY IDEA: Alerts & MonAlert recipes  
WHY IT MATTERS: Saves time and prevents missed entries or exits.  
HOW TO APPLY: Right‑click a price level → “Trap Alert Above High” or “Below Low.” Use preset recipes (e.g., “Buy‑Risk,” “No‑Nos”) to automatically flag violations, extensions, or ANTS confirmations.

KEY IDEA: Violation / No‑No analysis  
WHY IT MATTERS: Identifies abnormal behavior after you’re in a trade (e.g., close below 20‑day MA, multiple lower lows).  
HOW TO APPLY: After entering, switch MonAlert to “No‑Nos.” If red bars appear, consider exiting or reducing position.

KEY IDEA: Risk‑to‑Reward (R:R) minimum 2:1  
WHY IT MATTERS: Ensures each trade has the potential to earn at least twice what you risk.  
HOW TO APPLY: Calculate stop distance, then set target at ≥ 2 × stop. Use the platform’s risk calculator to verify before placing the trade.

KEY IDEA: Stop‑to‑Break‑Even financing  
WHY IT MATTERS: Allows you to lock in a risk‑free position by selling a portion of the trade.  
HOW TO APPLY: Determine the % of shares needed to cover the stop (e.g., 22 % for a 3.4 R trade). Set a sell order for that portion at the entry price.

KEY IDEA: Post‑trade analytics (Trade Grader, Analytics)  
WHY IT MATTERS: Objective performance review speeds learning and eliminates emotional bias.  
HOW TO APPLY: After closing, the platform auto‑sends the trade to Trade Grader. Review the grade, compare to your own expectations, and adjust future entries accordingly.

KEY IDEA: Market‑wide pressure and distribution vs. accumulation  
WHY IT MATTERS: The S&P 500 can be high while the majority of stocks are under pressure—sign of a fragile rally.  
HOW TO APPLY: Monitor the “% of stocks under pressure” indicator. If > 60 % are under pressure while the index makes new highs, stay in cash or look for high‑RPR, low‑pressure stocks.

KEY IDEA: Seasonal edge (December “Santa Claus” rally)  
WHY IT MATTERS: Historical data shows December often provides extra upside.  
HOW TO APPLY: In late November/early December, increase position size modestly on high‑quality setups, but still respect stop‑loss rules.

KEY IDEA: Avoid buying on 50‑day pullbacks alone  
WHY IT MATTERS: Pullbacks to the 50‑day MA are not a reliable entry unless a VCP base is present.  
HOW TO APPLY: Only consider a 50‑day pullback if the price is forming a tight right‑side contraction and the Fab‑5 panel is green.

KEY IDEA: Backup plan for connectivity  
WHY IT MATTERS: Internet outages can prevent you from managing stops.  
HOW TO APPLY: Keep a phone‑based trading app ready, have broker’s speed‑dial saved, and know your stop levels so you can verbally instruct a sell if needed.

SECTION 2: MINERVINI TRADING CONCEPTS EXTRACTED
================================================================================

- VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern): 3‑5 lower lows, decreasing volume, tight right‑side; entry just above breakout high.  
- Stage‑2 Uptrend: Stock > 25 % above 52‑week low, above 200‑day MA, trending up for several months.  
- Fab‑5 Panel: RPR, TPR, Pressure, Trend, Buy‑Risk; all green = strong candidate.  
- RPR (Relative Performance Rank): 1‑100 scale of relative strength; ≥ 90 preferred.  
- TPR (Technical Profile Rank): Grades A‑D; A/B are ideal, C acceptable with other green Fab‑5 signals, D off‑limits.  
- Pressure Indicator: Shows short‑term buyer/seller dominance; should turn green quickly after breakout.  
- Power Play: Stock doubles (or more) in ≤ 8 weeks, high RPR, tight base; fundamentals less critical.  
- Buy‑Risk: Early‑turn version of TPR; allows slightly looser criteria for early entries.  
- No‑Nos / Violations: Post‑entry red flags (close below 20‑day MA, multiple lower lows, etc.).  
- ANTS Confirmation: After breakout, a series of strong days confirming the move.  
- Angle Warning: Detects steep 50‑day MA angles that can impede a pullback; minor vs. major warnings.  
- Volume Run Rate (VRR): Daily volume vs. 50‑day average; low VRR on right side is desirable.  
- Alerts (Trap Alert): Quick one‑click alerts for price levels (above high, below low).  
- MonAlert Recipes: Customizable sets of indicators (e.g., “Buy‑Risk,” “No‑Nos”) applied forward or backward.  
- Earnings Calendar Integration: Shows upcoming earnings directly on screen; helps avoid unwanted earnings risk.  
- Market Pressure Indicator: % of stocks under pressure; divergence from index signals hidden weakness.  
- Seasonal Patterns: December “Santa Claus” rally tendency.  
- Position Sizing & Risk per Trade: Typically 1‑2 % of portfolio risk per trade.  
- Stop‑to‑Break‑Even Financing: Sell a portion of shares to cover stop loss.  
- Trade Grader & Analytics: Automatic post‑trade grading and statistical tracking.  
- Model Book / Workbook: Collection of historic chart precedents for visual pattern matching.  
- IPO Screen: Finds newly listed stocks; requires extra caution because TPR may be unavailable.  
- Short‑selling Rules: Only short stocks below 200‑day MA, heavy down pressure, and with red Fab‑5.  
- Psychological Discipline: Earn the right to play, avoid chasing, stick to rules, accept losses quickly.

SECTION 3: TRADING RULES & DO'S/DON'TS
================================================================================

DO:
- Run the Bread & Butter screen every pre‑market and add tight, low‑volume pullbacks to your WATCH LIST.
- Verify Fab‑5 panel is green (RPR ≥ 90, TPR = A/B, Pressure off, Trend = Stage‑2, Buy‑Risk green) before entering.
- Enter only at the breakout high or a buy‑stop placed a few cents above it.
- Place stop loss just below the pivot low (or below the recent low of the right side).
- Ensure R:R ≥ 2:1; use the risk calculator to confirm.
- Finance your stop by selling the calculated % of shares (e.g., 22 % for a 3.4 R trade).
- Use MonAlert “Trap Alert” for entry, stop, and critical alerts.
- Review each closed trade in Trade Grader and adjust future entries.
- Keep a backup trading method (phone app, broker speed‑dial) for internet outages.
- Monitor the market‑wide pressure indicator; stay cautious when > 60 % of stocks are under pressure.

DON’T:
- Buy a stock that is merely pulling back to the 50‑day MA without a VCP base.
- Enter a trade if any Fab‑5 component is red (especially RPR < 70 or TPR = D).
- Chase a stock that has already moved 5 %+ above the pivot; wait for the next pullback.
- Short a stock that is in a confirmed uptrend or above its 200‑day MA.
- Ignore violations; a single red “No‑No” bar should trigger an exit or position reduction.
- Over‑size positions; never risk more than 2 % of your total capital on a single trade.
- Rely solely on price targets; let the chart dictate exits.
- Trade penny stocks (< $10) unless they meet power‑play criteria and have a clear base.
- Spend more than 30 minutes a day on screening without a focused list; avoid analysis paralysis.

SECTION 4: SETUP IDENTIFICATION GUIDE (Based on Transcript)
================================================================================

CHART CUES
- Daily chart shows 3‑5 lower lows, each lower low accompanied by lower volume.
- Right side of the pattern is tight (price range ≤ 10 % of recent high) and volume is at least 30‑80 % below 50‑day average.
- Pivot line drawn from the lowest low to the breakout high; price respects this line for at least 2‑3 days.
- Weekly chart confirms Stage‑2 uptrend (price > 25 % above 52‑week low, above 200‑day MA).

PRICE / VOLUME BEHAVIOR
- Volume contraction on each pullback day.
- After breakout, volume spikes sharply then returns to low levels.
- No‑Nos: No close below 20‑day MA, no more than 1 lower low after entry, no series of red closes.

BUY POINTS
- Place a buy‑stop a few cents (0.5‑1 %) above the breakout high or the prior day’s high.
- For intraday, use the high of the last bar of the right‑side contraction.
- Confirm with MonAlert “Buy‑Risk” green and Pressure turning green within 1‑2 days.

CONFIRMATION SIGNALS
- Pressure indicator turns green within 2 days of breakout.
- ANTS confirmation: ≥ 3 consecutive days with close above open and low‑to‑high range expanding.
- RPR jumps 2‑5 points on the day of breakout.
- TPR upgrades to A or B within the next week.

DANGER SIGNS / INVALIDATIONS
- Red “No‑Nos” appear (close below 20‑day MA, multiple lower lows, volume surge on down days).
- Pressure stays red for more than 2 days after breakout.
- RPR falls below 80 or TPR drops to C/D.
- Stock breaks below the pivot low before the stop is hit (indicates supply still present).
- Market‑wide pressure > 60 % while index makes new highs (high risk of hidden weakness).
