SECTION 1: STUDENT NOTES (Clear, Simple, Actionable)
================================================================================

KEY IDEA 1 – ONLY TRADE IN A LONG‑TERM UPTREND
Why it matters: If the market or the stock is not in a confirmed uptrend, all other fundamentals become irrelevant – you will likely lose money.
How to apply: Start every scan by checking the 200‑day moving average (or higher) on the index and on the individual stock. If the price is below that line, discard the ticker.

KEY IDEA 2 – USE A HIERARCHY OF QUALIFIERS
Why it matters: A step‑by‑step filter removes weak candidates and leaves only the strongest setups.
How to apply: Follow the three‑step order:
1. Long‑term trend (uptrend on the index and on the stock).
2. Current price action (higher lows, divergence from the market, new‑high dots).
3. Low‑risk entry (breakout of a base, pull‑back to a support zone, or a “follow‑through” day).

KEY IDEA 3 – NEW HIGHS ARE A POSITIVE SIGNAL, NOT A WARNING
Why it matters: A stock cannot make a large move without creating new all‑time highs. The money that fuels big moves lives on those highs.
How to apply: Look for black dots (all‑time highs) on the chart. When a stock is making a new high while the market is still low, it is a leader. Buy on the breakout or on a small pull‑back to the breakout level.

KEY IDEA 4 – FOCUS ON MARKET LEADERS DURING CORRECTIONS
Why it matters: 96 % of the biggest winners emerge from market corrections. Leaders diverge from the market, make higher lows, and eventually break out.
How to apply: Scan for stocks that:
– Are near or at 52‑week highs (within 5‑15 % of the high).
– Have a Relative Strength (RS) line that is flat or rising while the market is falling.
– Show higher lows while the index is making lower lows.

KEY IDEA 5 – PREDICTIVE, COINCIDENT, CONFIRMING SETUPS
Why it matters: Timing the entry depends on how early the leader appears.
How to apply:
– Predictive: Stock breaks out before the market bottom. Enter as soon as the base breakout is confirmed.
– Coincident: Stock breaks out around the same time the market makes a low. Use a “follow‑through” day as confirmation.
– Confirming: Stock breaks out after the market has already turned. Wait for a second day of higher volume to confirm.

KEY IDEA 6 – RELATIVE STRENGTH IS YOUR COMPASS
Why it matters: RS isolates the stock’s performance from the market. A rising RS while the market is down signals true strength.
How to apply: Plot the RS line (stock price divided by index price). Look for a line that is flat or trending up, especially when the index is trending down.

KEY IDEA 7 – POSITION SIZE & RISK CONTROL
Why it matters: Even the best setups can fail. Limiting loss protects capital and keeps you in the game.
How to apply:
– Max loss per trade ≤ 1 % of total account.
– Stop‑loss just below the recent swing low or below the base support.
– Reduce size on cyclical or less‑understood categories (biotech, turn‑arounds) until you have proven the pattern.

KEY IDEA 8 – DISCIPLINE OVER EMOTION
Why it matters: Undisciplined traders stay in losing positions, miss the next leader, and erode confidence.
How to apply: When a trade hits the stop, exit immediately. Do not “average down.” Keep a journal of every trade and review it daily.

KEY IDEA 9 – LEVERAGE COMES FROM BUYING ON NEW HIGHS
Why it matters: Buying a leader as it makes a new high gives you built‑in upside; the stock can move many multiples of the initial risk.
How to apply: After the breakout, let the trade run. Add to the position only if the stock makes a higher low and stays above the breakout level.

KEY IDEA 10 – CONTINUOUS LEARNING & REPEATS
Why it matters: The patterns repeat across decades (1990, 2000, 2008, 2020). Recognizing the repeat builds confidence.
How to apply: Study historic examples (Walmart 1982, Amazon 2001, Netflix 2008, NVIDIA 2020) and compare them to today’s charts. Keep a “pattern library” in your journal.

SECTION 3: TRADING RULES & DO'S/DON'TS
================================================================================

DO:
- Verify the long‑term uptrend on both the index and the stock before any other analysis.
- Scan for stocks within 5‑15 % of their 52‑week high during market corrections.
- Use the RS line; buy only when RS is flat or rising while the market is falling.
- Look for all‑time‑high dots (black dots) as confirmation of new‑high ground.
- Enter on a clean breakout of a base or on a pull‑back to the breakout level.
- Place a stop‑loss just below the most recent swing low or base support.
- Risk no more than 1 % of your account on any single trade.
- Reduce position size for cyclical, biotech, or turnaround stocks until you have proven the setup.
- Keep a trade journal; review every trade after market close.
- Stay out of the market when you are emotionally compromised; be ready to act when the next leader appears.

DON’T:
- Trade a stock that is below its 200‑day moving average.
- Buy a stock that is far from its 52‑week high (more than 25 % down) during a correction.
- Chase a stock after it has already made a large new high; wait for the next pull‑back.
- Hold a losing position hoping it will turn around; exit at the stop.
- Increase size on a losing trade to “average down.”
- Ignore institutional footprints; avoid stocks with no clear institutional buying.
- Rely solely on earnings for biotech; they often have no earnings – use pure chart analysis instead.
- Enter a trade without a clear entry rule (breakout, pull‑back, follow‑through day).
- Let emotions dictate trade size or timing.

SECTION 4: SETUP IDENTIFICATION GUIDE (Based on Transcript)
================================================================================

CHART CUES
- Black dots = all‑time highs. A cluster of new dots signals “new‑high ground.”
- Higher lows while the index is making lower lows = divergence, a leader.
- Base patterns (cup‑with‑handle, flat base, V‑base) that are forming in an uptrend.
- A “follow‑through” day: strong volume, price closes above the breakout level.

PRICE / VOLUME BEHAVIOR
- Volume spikes on breakout or on the follow‑through day.
- Low volume pull‑backs to the breakout level are ideal entry points.
- After breakout, volume should stay above the average of the breakout day.

BUY POINTS
- At the breakout candle’s high (enter on the close of the breakout).
- On a pull‑back to the breakout level (enter at the low of the pull‑back).
- On the first day of a follow‑through after a base breakout.

CONFIRMATION SIGNALS
- RS line flat or rising while the market is falling.
- Institutional buying footprints (large block trades, rising OBV).
- Price staying above the 50‑day MA after entry.

DANGER SIGNS / INVALIDATIONS
- Stock falls back below the breakout level within 2‑3 days.
- RS line turns sharply down while the market is still down.
- New high dots stop appearing for more than 4‑6 weeks.
- Volume dries up on pull‑backs, indicating lack of buying interest.
- The stock breaks below its 200‑day MA after entry.

SECTION 5: PRACTICAL DAILY ACTION PLAN FOR ME
================================================================================

PRE‑MARKET CHECKLIST
- Review the index’s 200‑day MA; confirm market is in an uptrend or at least not in a deep downtrend.
- Scan for stocks within 5‑15 % of 52‑week high and above their 200‑day MA.
- Pull up RS charts; filter for RS flat or rising.
- Identify all‑time‑high dots on the daily chart.
- Note any upcoming earnings or catalyst events; avoid biotech without earnings unless you have a pure chart case.
- Prepare a watchlist of 8‑12 candidates that meet all three qualifiers.

MARKET HOURS DISCIPLINE
- Monitor the watchlist for breakout candles or pull‑backs to breakout levels.
- When a breakout occurs, verify volume > 1.5× average and RS staying flat/rising.
- Enter with position size = (1 % of account) / (distance from entry to stop).
- Set stop‑loss immediately below swing low or base support.
- Trail stop to just below new higher lows as the trade moves in your favor.
- Avoid adding to a losing position; only add on a clear higher‑low confirmation.

POST‑MARKET REVIEW TASKS
- Log every trade: entry, stop, exit, reason, emotions.
- Review charts for any missed setups (stocks that made new highs but you didn’t trade).
- Update the watchlist: remove stocks that failed qualifiers, add new candidates.
- Calculate P/L and ensure total daily loss ≤ 2 % of account (max two losing trades per day).
- Reflect on discipline: did you exit at stop? Did you stick to the plan?

RISK CONTROL REMINDERS
- Max 1 % risk per trade, never exceed 2 % total daily loss.
- Never move stop loss farther away after entry.
- Keep position size smaller for cyclical/biotech/turn‑around stocks (½ % risk).
- Use a hard stop; no “mental stops.”

MINDSET REINFORCEMENT (Mark style)
- Remind yourself: “The market is a voting machine; the stock is a pricing machine.”
- Visualize the “new‑high ground” concept – money lives there.
- Accept that losers happen; focus on the 96 % of winners that emerge from corrections.
- Stay patient during market panic; the best opportunities appear when everyone is scared.
- Repeat the mantra: “Qualify, qualify, qualify – then execute with precision.”
