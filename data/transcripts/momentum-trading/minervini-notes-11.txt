SECTION 1: STUDENT NOTES (Clear, Simple, Actionable)
================================================================================

KEY IDEA 1 – CONTEXT IS EVERYTHING  
Why it matters: Every trade must be viewed inside the larger market‑trend, earnings‑growth, and sector‑dynamic picture. Without context a good chart pattern can be a trap.  
How to apply: Start each analysis with the “satellite view” (overall market trend), then zoom to “aerial view” (sector/industry), finally to “overhead view” (individual stock chart). Only move to the next level if the higher‑level context is favorable.

KEY IDEA 2 – CATEGORIZE COMPANIES BEFORE YOU BUY  
Why it matters: Different categories (market leader, top competitor, institutional favorite, turnaround, cyclicals, commodity) have different risk/reward profiles and require different entry criteria.  
How to apply: When a stock appears on your screen, first decide which bucket it belongs to. Use the bucket’s specific checklist (e.g., turnarounds need triple‑digit earnings growth; cyclicals need tighter risk limits).

KEY IDEA 3 – THE THREE CORE GOALS OF MINERVINI’S ENTRY PROCESS  
Why it matters: The whole system is built to (1) minimize risk, (2) capture outsized upside, (3) compound returns efficiently.  
How to apply:  
1. Set a stop‑loss that limits loss to a small % of your account (typically 1‑2%).  
2. Look for stocks that can move many times the risk amount (high “leverage” from price action, not from margin).  
3. Turn capital over quickly – don’t stay in cash longer than necessary, but also don’t be over‑exposed (Mark is ~50% invested on average).

KEY IDEA 4 – HARD FILTERS THAT NEVER BREAK  
Why it matters: The 200‑day moving average (MA) rule alone blocks >99% of losers. It is non‑negotiable.  
How to apply: Never buy a stock whose 200‑day MA is sloping down or whose price is below that MA. If you see a breakout but the 200‑day is down, walk away.

KEY IDEA 5 – RELATIVE STRENGTH (RS) AS A QUICK SCREEN  
Why it matters: 90% of historic winners have RS > 68 (IBD‑style). Low RS stocks rarely become big winners.  
How to apply: Use the built‑in RS indicator on Minervini Markets 360 (or IBD RS). Reject any candidate below 68.

KEY IDEA 6 – FUNDAMENTAL QUALIFIERS THAT ARE NOT NEGOTIABLE  
Why it matters: Strong earnings acceleration, small float, recent IPO, and a clear catalyst are the statistical hallmarks of winners.  
How to apply:  
- Earnings growth ≥ 20‑30% QoQ for the last 6‑8 quarters.  
- Float ≤ 50‑100 M (adjust upward for later eras).  
- Recent IPO (within 5‑10 years) or a clear “new product/technology” catalyst.  
If any of these is missing, lower the stock’s priority.

KEY IDEA 7 – STYLE DRIFT IS THE REAL KILLER  
Why it matters: Jumping from market leaders to cyclicals to “hot” strategies erodes skill development.  
How to apply: Commit to one style (e.g., market‑leader/turnaround) for at least 6‑12 months. Use a checklist to keep you from buying a “trend” that doesn’t fit your chosen category.

KEY IDEA 8 – MINIMAL USE OF MARGIN  
Why it matters: Mark’s 40‑year record shows massive returns with little or no margin. Leverage should come from high‑alpha stocks, not borrowed money.  
How to apply: Trade with cash or a small, judicious margin line only on intraday contests. For daily/weekly trading, keep margin at 0%.

KEY IDEA 9 – NOTE‑TAKING IS PART OF THE LEARNING LOOP    
How to apply: Write questions directly in the workbook, annotate charts, and review your notes after each session.

KEY IDEA 10 – MINDSET: BELIEF + DISCIPLINE = CONSISTENT EDGE  
Why it matters: Confidence in the process protects you from emotional shortcuts.  
How to apply:  
- Keep an open mind, especially when the market feels “too easy.”  
- Remind yourself daily that the system works over decades, not weeks.  
- Visualize the “fire‑hose” of information as a structured flow, not chaos.

- FACTOR MODELING – Study of historical winners to extract repeatable traits (the “gold medalist” analogy).

- COMMODITY PLAY – Only when margin expansion is sustainable; otherwise avoid.  
- SCREENING PIPELINE – From ~9,000 stocks → ~1,000 with basic filters → ~50‑100 with deeper analysis → 1‑3 daily trades.  
- PSYCHOLOGY – Open mind, belief in process, commitment, note‑taking, avoid “quick‑fix” shortcuts.

SECTION 3: TRADING RULES & DO'S/DON'TS
================================================================================

DO:
- Write every question in the workbook with the page number.  
- Watch every session; each builds on the previous.  
- Keep the 200‑day MA rule intact – never buy below it.  
- Use RS > 68 as a minimum strength filter.  
- Look for earnings acceleration and a clear catalyst before entering.  
- Trade only stocks priced $10+ (avoid penny stocks).  
- Keep margin usage at zero for regular trading.  
- Limit overall portfolio exposure to ~50% of capital.  
- Size each trade to risk no more than 1‑2% of account equity.  
- Review and annotate charts immediately after each trade.  
- Maintain a single trading style for at least six months.  

DON’T:
- Post questions in the Zoom chat during the presentation.   
- Buy a stock that is below its 200‑day moving average, even if the story is exciting.  
- Trade based solely on hype or “hot” tips without the full checklist.  
- Use large amounts of margin to chase returns.  
- Trade cyclicals without tighter risk limits and smaller position sizes.  
- Let style drift pull you into unrelated strategies.  
- Ignore the need for a catalyst; a stock with strong fundamentals but no upcoming event is a lower‑probability play.  
- Over‑trade; aim for a few high‑conviction entries per day.

SECTION 4: SETUP IDENTIFICATION GUIDE (Based on Transcript)
================================================================================

CHART CUES
- Price breaking above a recent base (VCP‑like contraction) and forming a new “high‑ground” (new 52‑week high).  
- Volume spike on breakout (2‑3× average daily volume).  
- RS indicator climbing above 68 and staying there.  
- 200‑day MA sloping upward, price above it; 50‑day MA also upward.  

PRICE / VOLUME BEHAVIOR
- Minimal pullback after breakout (tight stop‑loss placement).  
- Consistent upward momentum on daily/weekly charts.  
- Float is small enough to allow rapid price moves (check share count).  

BUY POINTS
- Enter on the first close above the breakout level with volume confirmation.  
- Place stop‑loss just below the breakout point or below the 200‑day MA, whichever is tighter.  

CONFIRMATION SIGNALS
- Recent earnings beat or announced catalyst (new product, acquisition, spinoff).  
- Earnings acceleration: QoQ EPS growth >20% for at least 3‑4 quarters.  
- Relative Strength >68 for at least 4‑6 weeks.  

DANGER SIGNS / INVALIDATIONS
- Price falls back below the 200‑day MA.  
- RS drops below 68 after entry.  
- No catalyst or earnings are flat/declining.  
- Float is large (>200 M) and price action is sluggish.  
- Market enters a strong downtrend (e.g., S&P down >15%); avoid new entries.

SECTION 5: PRACTICAL DAILY ACTION PLAN FOR ME
================================================================================

PRE‑MARKET CHECKLIST
- Review macro: S&P trend, sector rotation, major news.  
- Run screen: price > $10, above 200‑day MA, RS >68, float < 50 M, earnings QoQ growth >20% (last 4 quarters).  
- Identify any upcoming catalysts (earnings releases, product launches, regulatory approvals).  
- Update watchlist in Minervini Markets 360; annotate any new chart patterns.  
- Set alerts for breakout levels on top 3‑5 candidates.  

MARKET HOURS DISCIPLINE
- Monitor alerts; when a breakout occurs, verify volume and RS before entering.  
- Execute trade at the first close above breakout; set stop‑loss immediately.  
- Keep position size to risk ≤2% of account equity.  
- Avoid any trade that would push total exposure >50% of capital.  
- Do not use margin; trade with cash only.  
- Record entry price, stop‑loss, rationale, and chart screenshot in workbook.  
- Stay away from chat for questions; write them down for Q&A session.  

POST‑MARKET REVIEW TASKS
- Close out any positions that hit stop‑loss or target.  
- Journal each trade: what worked, what didn’t, any rule breaches.  
- Update the workbook with lessons learned and any unanswered questions (page #).  
- Re‑run the screen to refresh the watchlist for the next day.  
- Review overall portfolio exposure; rebalance if >50% invested.  

RISK CONTROL REMINDERS
- Never buy below the 200‑day MA.  
- Stop‑loss must be placed before the trade is entered.  
- Max loss per trade = 2% of account.  
- No more than 3 new entries per day.  
- Avoid margin; if a contest forces margin, limit it to <10% of position size and only for intraday.  

MINDSET REINFORCEMENT (Mark style)
- Read a short excerpt from “Trade Like a Stock Market Wizard” each morning.  
- Recite the three core goals: minimal risk, big upside, fast compounding.  
- Visualize the “satellite → aerial → overhead” flow before scanning stocks.  
- Remind yourself: “The system works over decades, not days.”
