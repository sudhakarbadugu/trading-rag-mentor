SECTION 1: STUDENT NOTES (Clear, Simple, Actionable)
================================================================================

KEY IDEA: POWER PLAY = HIGH‑TIGHT FLAG (O’NEILL STYLE)
WHY IT MATTERS: It signals a rapid, explosive move (≈100%+ gain) from a dormant stock, offering huge alpha.
HOW TO APPLY: Scan for stocks that have been flat for 10‑12 days (max 20% swing for mid‑price, 25% for low‑price), then explode ≥95% in ≤8 weeks.

KEY IDEA: VELOCITY PLAY vs. MAGNITUDE PLAY
WHY IT MATTERS: Velocity plays start with fast price acceleration; magnitude plays let you enter earlier, before the full move.
HOW TO APPLY: Look for the first “velocity” breakout (tightening price, 200‑day turning up) and consider a partial entry; add on as the base tightens.

KEY IDEA: RELATIVE STRENGTH (RS) & 200‑DAY MOVING AVERAGE
WHY IT MATTERS: RS above the market and a 200‑day in an uptrend confirm a leader.
HOW TO APPLY: Require the 200‑day to be above the 50‑day for at least 30 days (or loosen for dormant stocks). If the 200‑day is flat/down, still consider a “early turn” if the stock is exploding.

KEY IDEA: SHALLOW PULLBACK IS CRUCIAL
WHY IT MATTERS: Small pullbacks (≤10‑15%) indicate little supply and strong institutional accumulation.
HOW TO APPLY: After the breakout, measure the max retracement. If it exceeds ~20% (or 25% for cheap stocks) reject or treat as lower‑probability.

KEY IDEA: SUPPLY‑DEMAND DYNAMICS
WHY IT MATTERS: A stock that rockets without profit‑taking shows that institutions are still buying.
HOW TO APPLY: Observe volume on the breakout; low profit‑taking volume + minimal pullback = high confidence.

KEY IDEA: DISCIPLINE THROUGH SCREENING
WHY IT MATTERS: Filtering out 90%+ of symbols removes noise and prevents “candy” (bad ideas).
HOW TO APPLY: Build a screen that enforces: (1) RS > market, (2) 200‑day up/flat, (3) base length 10‑12 days, (4) max pullback ≤20%, (5) price‑action tightening.

KEY IDEA: POSITION SIZING & STOP‑LOSS
WHY IT MATTERS: Even a perfect setup can be wrecked by a single large loss.
HOW TO APPLY: Risk ≤1% of account per trade; set stop 5% below entry (or tighter if volatility is low). For power plays, give a little extra room (5‑7%) because you expect fast moves.

KEY IDEA: TRADE MANAGEMENT – HOLDING FOR 20‑50 DAYS
WHY IT MATTERS: The biggest moves often occur after the first 5‑10 days.
HOW TO APPLY: After a winning breakout, keep at least a portion of the position for 20‑50 days, scaling out gradually (e.g., 30% at 20% profit, 30% at 40%, rest to target).

KEY IDEA: JUMPY VS. COMPLACENT TRADERS
WHY IT MATTERS: Jumpies get stopped out on normal pullbacks; complacents hold through big violations.
HOW TO APPLY: Know the “normal” pullback range for the stock; exit only when pullback exceeds your rule (e.g., >20% for mid‑price).

KEY IDEA: REVERSE‑RECOVERY (SQUAT) PATTERN
WHY IT MATTERS: A brief dip below the breakout level that quickly recovers is a sign of strong accumulation.
HOW TO APPLY: If a squat lasts 1‑2 days and the stock rebounds above the breakout high, stay in; if it lingers >3 days, consider exiting.

KEY IDEA: CONTINUOUS LEARNING & ART OF PRICE ACTION
WHY IT MATTERS: Rules are a framework; the real edge comes from reading supply‑demand nuances.
HOW TO APPLY: Review every trade, note deviations, and refine your screen and entry criteria over time.

SECTION 2: MINERVINI TRADING CONCEPTS EXTRACTED
================================================================================

- POWER PLAY (high‑tight flag): ≥95% move in ≤8 weeks, shallow pullback, originates from a flat base.
- VELOCITY PLAY: Early breakout with rapid price acceleration; entry before full base formation.
- MAGNITUDE PLAY: Entry at the earliest possible point, often at the start of the velocity move.
- VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern): Series of tightening price ranges; each contraction should be tighter than the last.
- BASE CONSOLIDATION: 10‑12 days of sideways movement, max 20% swing (25% for <$5 stocks).
- RELATIVE STRENGTH (RS): Stock’s performance vs. market; must be strong to qualify.
- 200‑DAY MOVING AVERAGE (MA): Should be turning up or at least flat; early turn acceptable for dormant stocks.
- 50‑DAY ABOVE 200‑DAY: Confirmation of stage‑2 uptrend (30‑day minimum).
- SHALLOW PULLBACK RULE: ≤10‑15% ideal, ≤20% maximum; deeper pullbacks indicate supply.
- SUPPLY‑DEMAND ANALYSIS: Low profit‑taking volume + minimal pullback = institutional accumulation.
- STOP‑LOSS: Typically 5% below entry; may be widened to 5‑7% for power plays.
- POSITION SIZING: Risk ≤1% of capital per trade.
- TRADE MANAGEMENT: Scale out over 20‑50 days; hold a core position for the full move.
- SQUAT / REVERSE RECOVERY: Brief dip after breakout; 1‑2 day recovery is a green flag.
- DISCIPLINE THROUGH SCREENING: Filter out 80‑90% of symbols; focus on top 5‑10% that meet criteria.
- PSYCHOLOGY: Avoid “candy” (temptation to chase); be the last‑week holder, not the first‑week holder.
- RULE FLEXIBILITY: Stretch rules only when the statistical edge remains (e.g., early turn without full stage‑2).
- LONG‑TERM vs. SHORT‑TERM: Power plays can be held longer (20‑50 days) for swing profits; other setups may be day‑trades.

SECTION 3: TRADING RULES & DO'S/DON'TS
================================================================================

DO:
- Screen daily and only look at symbols that meet all base, RS, and MA criteria.
- Enter on the first tight breakout (velocity play) with a stop 5‑7% below entry.
- Keep pullback depth ≤20% (≤15% ideal) before adding or staying in.
- Scale out gradually; keep at least 30‑40% of the position for 20‑50 days.
- Use a maximum risk of 1% of account per trade.
- Review each trade after market close; note what matched or violated the rules.
- Stay patient; wait for the full 10‑12 day base before entering if you missed the early turn.
- Adjust stop size for low‑price stocks (allow up to 25% pullback tolerance).
- Keep a “cheat” area in mind – buying on a low‑price cheat can improve average cost if supply is low.
- Maintain discipline: no trading stocks that do not meet the checklist, even if they look “hot”.

DON'T:
- Chase a stock that has already moved >200% in the past 5 years (late‑stage blow‑off).
- Enter a power play if the pullback exceeds the max allowed (20‑25%).
- Trade a stock whose 200‑day is in a clear downtrend unless you have a strong early‑turn justification.
- Sell the entire position as soon as you hit a 20% profit on a power play.
- Let a squat last more than 2‑3 days without a clear recovery.
- Over‑tighten stops (e.g., 1‑2%) on volatile power plays; you’ll get “death by a thousand cuts.”
- Ignore the market context; a breakout in a strong market pullback is a red flag.
- Stretch the rules just because you “feel” a stock is special; stick to the statistical edge.
- Trade on news alone without confirming the price‑action pattern.
- Let emotions dictate position size; always calculate risk before entering.

SECTION 4: SETUP IDENTIFICATION GUIDE (Based on Transcript)
================================================================================

CHART CUES
- Flat consolidation for 10‑12 days, price range ≤20% (≤25% for <$5 stocks).
- Tightening candlesticks (VCP) with each contraction narrower than the previous.
- 200‑day MA beginning to turn up or already above the 50‑day for ≥30 days.
- Relative Strength line diverging upward from the market index.
- Volume spike on breakout, then tapering off (low profit‑taking).

PRICE / VOLUME BEHAVIOR
- Breakout candle closes above the high of the base with volume ≥1.5‑2× average.
- Immediate pullback after breakout should be ≤10‑15% of breakout size.
- Minimal volume on pullback; no large sell‑off bars.
- After pullback, price resumes upward with 1‑3% daily gains (tennis‑ball action).

BUY POINTS
- Primary entry: at the breakout high (or slightly above) with stop 5‑7% below.
- Secondary entry (cheat): on a low‑price “cheat” within the base if supply appears low.
- Add‑on: after a successful pullback, re‑enter on a tighter VCP contraction.

CONFIRMATION SIGNALS
- 200‑day staying above 50‑day (or early turn confirmed by price staying above 200).
- RS staying above market for at least 3 consecutive days.
- Pullback depth ≤10% and recovery within 1‑2 days (reverse‑recovery).
- Tight weekly closes (3‑5 consecutive weeks with <0.5% range) reinforce strength.

DANGER SIGNS / INVALIDATIONS
- Pullback >20% (or >25% for cheap stocks) – reject or exit.
- 200‑day moving down sharply, crossing below 50‑day.
- Breakout occurs during a market-wide crash and the stock fails to hold above the breakout high.
- Volume surge on pullback (indicates profit‑taking).
- Squat lasting >3 days without a clear bounce.
- RS dropping below market for two consecutive days.

SECTION 5: PRACTICAL DAILY ACTION PLAN FOR ME
================================================================================

PRE‑MARKET CHECKLIST:
- Run the master screen: RS > market, 200‑day up/flat, base length 10‑12 days, max swing ≤20%.
- Review watchlist for any new “early turn” candidates (200‑day just turned up).
- Check macro context: overall market trend, upcoming earnings, major news.
- Set alerts for price approaching breakout levels and for pullback depth.
- Verify position‑size calculator (risk 1% of equity, stop 5‑7% below entry).

MARKET HOURS DISCIPLINE:
- Monitor breakout candidates; only enter after confirming volume spike and price above high.
- Place stop order immediately after entry; do not move stop tighter than 5% unless volatility justifies.
- If a squat occurs, watch for 1‑2 day recovery; do not exit on the first dip.
- Scale out: 30% at 20% profit, another 30% at 40% profit, rest to target (50‑100%+).
- Keep a journal note on each trade (entry, stop, reason, emotions).

POST‑MARKET REVIEW TASKS:
- Review each trade against the checklist; mark any rule breaches.
- Record pullback depth, recovery time, and final profit/loss.
- Update the watchlist: move winners to “hold for 20‑50 days” list, losers to “review” list.
- Analyze any missed setups (stocks that met criteria but you didn’t trade) and note why.
- Adjust screen parameters if market conditions have shifted (e.g., widen pullback tolerance in high volatility).

RISK CONTROL REMINDERS:
- Never risk more than 1% of total capital on a single trade.
- Keep stops at least 5% away; for low‑price stocks allow up to 7% if volatility is low.
- If a trade hits stop, accept loss and move on – no revenge trading.
- Review daily max loss limit (e.g., 2% of account); stop trading for the day if reached.
- Use position‑sizing software to avoid manual errors.

MINDSET REINFORCEMENT (Mark style):
- “Be the last‑week holder, not the first‑week holder.”
- Treat each trade as a probability play; focus on edge, not on individual outcome.
- Remember the “train schedule” – if the stock isn’t arriving on time, it’s likely delayed.
- Accept that strategies have cycles; stay disciplined during down periods.
- Visualize the trade plan before entering; repeat the mantra: “Stick to the rules, let the edge work.”
